Story of OneFlora - Learning to 'Observe'​ surroundings

Around January 2018, out of no where, I found myself in a college classroom of one of the premier Botanical Institute in India - Agharkar Research Institute, Pune (ARI). It was an open for all, three month-weekend course on 'Field Botany' - specifically designed for commoners to be able to observe and identify plants and shrubs and herbs those we see all around.

Mandar Joshi 5 min. read


During those three months, we had many "Tree Walks" around Pune city. Although I been to those places before, viewing those places thru botanical "spectacles" was an altogether different experience. Same plants surrounding me till date, now are looking, behaving, communicating entirely different language.



Understanding 'Plant Morphology' was the first step. Recognizing different types of leaves, structure of inflorescence and various flower parts was a very intriguing and to an extent a very daunting task.



"Differentiation" is a key aspect in a process of 'Identifying' a plant. Bentham and Hooker classification system has categorized global plant species, based on floral part characters. There are about 3 Lac flowering plants in the world about 18000 in India. These numbers are enough to give a sense of complexity and intricacy involved in this classification process and to fathom the diversity in nature. Plants with similar floral characters are first grouped into 'Family' and then sub grouped into 'Genus' & 'Species'. Its like a funnel which spits out a plant name after sieving it thru hugely complicated filter.

This apparently simple logic to Identify plants can be tricky and exhaustive for amateur observer like myself. Contradictory information in various books and websites, difficult botanical terms and complex species descriptions in flora's, limited field observations and very little expert advise were some of the roadblocks. Still in most of case I could decipher the name and learned the tricks and trades.

After the course, I continued recording my own plant observations, scanning road sides, public gardens, canals, hills and just everything that looks green!! After about 3 years, I had a collection of about 700 species and about 6000 to 7000 photos.

IDing plants was like a treasure hunt with clues lying all over - requiring analytical ability, pattern recognition, acute observation and a huge patience.


Ever wondered how the flower of potato or carrot looks like? how does peanuts grow? Is apple or fig really a fruit? What is it exactly that the street vendor sold you as शमी leaves or गणेशपत्री ?

Slowly and gradually I started getting answers to such 'known unknown'.



The whole purpose of going thru this process of plant identification is to be able to understand what and how to 'Nurture'. That means, to be able to decide which plants to grow at which location and better understand their role in overall ecosystem. Planting tress without knowing their Identity, is like starting business without knowing the product!!

"Know Your Tree" is most important step before planting that tree. Unfortunately, KYT is ignored in most of the CRS, amateur or even Government driven planation drives. Actually when planting is considered for nature conservation, selecting saplings depends on many factors. Nature of the terrain, local whether and soil condition, purpose of plantation, water availability and many other factors drive the selection of the saplings and Identity is just one of those parameters.

Approach should be to move from tree plantation to 'Land Restoration' - restoring the land to its pre-industrial revolution state. Maintaining grass lands, mountain slops, forests and even deserts as such without trying to change their natural land profile. Giving enough consideration to insects, birds and mammals and build a healthy and sustainable space.

"Nurture" can only start after we Observe, Differentiate & Identify.


Observe >> Differentiate >> Identify >> Nurture
